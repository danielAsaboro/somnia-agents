# Last Wish Brand System

## Direction

Last Wish should read like a modern heirloom product: ceremonial in posture, blunt in claims, archival in structure, and operational once the desk opens.

## Palette

- `lacquer-black`: `#090806`
- `lacquer-panel`: `#191612`
- `parchment`: `#f6f0e3`
- `worn-parchment`: `#efe5d4`
- `oxidized-brass`: `#b88b43`
- `deep-brass`: `#8c6429`
- `archival-green`: `#55786a`
- `archival-green-deep`: `#355348`
- `warning-red`: `#9f4739`

## Typography

- Display serif: hero statements, section headlines, dossier titles.
- Clean sans: controls, labels, explanatory copy, system text.
- Mono with tabular numerals: addresses, balances, chain IDs, request IDs, transaction hashes.

## Voice

- Grave, specific, restrained.
- Say what the system records, gates, verifies, or refuses.
- Avoid startup filler, hype, and vague claims like "secure", "seamless", or "revolutionary".
- Prefer legal-document tension over crypto-dashboard slang.

## Shape Language

- Ruled ledger lines.
- Plaque or seal motifs for status markers.
- Framed evidence windows instead of floating cards everywhere.
- Vertical dividers, dossier edges, and document insets.
- Occasional curved cutouts only when they break the grid with intent.

## Motion

- Subtle editorial motion only.
- Use opacity, transform, and parallax sparingly to clarify depth.
- No `transition: all`.
- Reduced motion must collapse cleanly to a static layout.

## State Copy

- Loading: "Reading the estate record."
- Empty: "No will loaded yet."
- Error: "Couldn't read this will."
- Wallet missing: "Read-only archive mode."
- Wallet present, disconnected: "Wallet present, desk unopened."
